ZAT ESIM(Noun)

A noun is the name of a thing. Who? What? answers the questions.

According to composition:

Single (Simply - one root) For example: field, head, pen.

Complex (Сольное - several roots) For example: address, kindergarten.

Basic (Neprovidnoe - net suffix) For example: school, cat.

Derivative (Proizvodnoye - есть suffix) For example: singer, cook.

According to meaning:

General (Feminine) For example: TV, table.

Private For example: Almaty, Tatyana, Kazakhstan.

By auxiliary nouns, we mean auxiliary words whose lexical meaning is sometimes preserved, sometimes faded, depending on the features of combination with other words, syntactically, sometimes they are used in the calculation of an individual member, sometimes in the calculation of a connecting element in a complex member, and morphologically, they are transformed into nouns. The function of a noun in a sentence The noun is the most abundant of the noun-word classes and the main source of other words. A noun interacts with other words and performs the function of all parts of a sentence in a sentence. However, since a noun expresses a material concept, its most important, main function in a sentence is the beginning and complement . Nouns can be determiners , qualifiers , and narrators depending on the word they are combined with and where they are in the sentence .

Plural nouns include the following nouns:
Names of liquid substances: kefir, vodka, boza, tea, sauce, etc. b.
Names of gaseous substances: ozone, steam, smoke, fog, etc. b.
Names of powdery substances, lumpy fine substances and fragmented, scattered, ground substances: flour, dust, pollen, fenugreek, wormwood, wool, bottom, hair, eyelashes, berries, cherries, etc. b.
Names of things that express abstract concepts: trick, gratitude, mind, dispute, grief, misery, support, truth, etc. b.
Names of lumps that do not naturally disintegrate by themselves: gold, bronze, armor, cast iron, etc. b.
Names of natural phenomena: blizzard, rain, snow, wind, whirlwind, etc. b.
